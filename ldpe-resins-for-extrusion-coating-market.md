<p><ul><li>Tubular Process</li><li>Autoclave Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>LDPE resins for extrusion coating are produced using two primary processes: the tubular process and the autoclave process. The tubular process involves polymerizing ethylene in a tubular reactor, resulting in high molecular weight, low-density polyethylene that offers excellent clarity and flexibility, ideal for packaging applications. In contrast, the autoclave process employs a stirred tank reactor to achieve a more controlled polymer structure, yielding resins with varying densities and properties, suitable for diverse coating applications including food packaging and protective films.</p></p>
