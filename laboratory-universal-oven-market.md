<p><ul><li>Natural Convection</li><li>Forced Convection</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Laboratory Universal Oven market is primarily divided into two types: natural convection and forced convection ovens. Natural convection ovens rely on the natural movement of heated air to distribute warmth, making them ideal for gentle drying and heating processes. In contrast, forced convection ovens use fans to circulate heated air, enabling faster and more uniform temperature control. This makes forced convection ovens suitable for applications requiring precise thermal management, such as drying samples or conducting specific experiments.</p></p>
