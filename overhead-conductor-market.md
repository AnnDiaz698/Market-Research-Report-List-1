<p><p>The overhead conductor market comprises two main types: Conventional Overhead Conductors and High-Temperature Overhead Conductors. Conventional overhead conductors are widely used for power transmission, characterized by aluminum or copper materials, typically operating at standard temperature limits. In contrast, High-Temperature Overhead Conductors are designed to withstand elevated temperatures, allowing for higher current capacities without sagging or thermal degradation. This advancement in material technology enhances grid efficiency and reliability, making High-Temperature conductors ideal for modern energy demands and expanding infrastructure needs.</p></p>
