<p><p>The contraceptive drugs market is categorized into three main types: long-acting, short-acting, and emergency contraceptives. Long-acting contraceptives, such as IUDs and implants, provide extended protection, often lasting several years with minimal maintenance. Short-acting contraceptives, including pills and injections, require regular administration and offer flexibility. Emergency contraceptives are designed for immediate use after unprotected intercourse to prevent pregnancy. Each type addresses specific needs and preferences, contributing to a comprehensive approach to reproductive health and family planning.</p></p>
