<p><p>The bionic exoskeletons market consists of two main types: active and passive exoskeletons. Active exoskeletons are powered by motors and sensors, providing dynamic assistance for mobility and strength enhancement, making them suitable for rehabilitation and industrial applications. In contrast, passive exoskeletons rely on mechanical structures and materials to support the wearer’s body, reducing fatigue and improving posture without powered assistance, primarily utilized in manual labor scenarios. Both types enhance physical capabilities and promote ergonomics in various environments.</p></p>
