<p><p>Asthma reliever medications, commonly known as rescue medications, are primarily categorized into two types: quick-relief medications and long-term control medications. Quick-relief medications, such as short-acting bronchodilators, provide immediate relief from acute asthma symptoms by relaxing airway muscles. Long-term control medications, including inhaled corticosteroids, aim to reduce inflammation and prevent asthma attacks over time. While quick-relief medications are used during asthma episodes, long-term control medications are essential for daily management and prevention of future attacks, ensuring better overall respiratory health.</p></p>
